The best way to invest your money

"Investment" has become something of a catch phrase in recent years.Although "investment" itself is nothing more than a process of acquiring money (by making investments). In this article, we will talk about, how to start investing your money correctly (so that it can bring you passive income).):1) I AM INVESTED IN THE NUMBERS.I understand that many people are.Because the interest on my investment now is several times higher than it was before the quarantine (which is why I am writing these words). 2) Diversification is KEY.Never invest all your money in just one (or two or three) instruments.3) The biggest risks to your money come from within.Take a closer look at the portfolios of some of the world's leading economies: China, India, and Portugal. Because they have a vested financial interest in maintaining a high level of service delivery in their citizens (they have banks and invest.companies). countries). 4) Market volatility is also a good teacher.Always think about what is happening in the economy and what may happen in the near future.It will be interesting to see how the value of various assets (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) will change over the coming months.5) Investment philosophy.The simplest rule will help you "make friends" with your money:after receiving a salary, immediately set aside 10-20% of the amount received (but do not keep this money for more than 6 months). this is called "liquidity optimization";after that, regularly rebalance your investments between different asset classes.If you are engaged in sales, then, as an option, sell what is rising in the price and then buy what is falling in price.This approach is called "index investing":the main advantage of index investing is that the shares you bought during the peak of the crisis in the financial markets were always the same price when the crisis ended;while in the depths of a financial crisis, the indices fell strongly. 6) Investment strategy.Now is the time to act strategically.Be sure to conduct an analysis of the programs that are currently in effect in your wallet (so that you can safely act in the current situation — even if the markets continue to fall and not rise).7) The best time to invest. is today.Yes, today there is no better time to invest (and to buy assets).But, as with any other investment, periodic investment is absolutely necessary. regularity > expense ratio.8) Stick to index investing.Although in the short term, index funds can sometimes be very expensive (in the extreme).Therefore, you should not consider them as an investment option in the near future.If you are willing to wait a little (until the stock market recovers and the financial crisis is over), you can start investing
